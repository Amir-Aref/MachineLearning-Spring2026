## Late Submission Policy

The late submission policy will be announced by the course staff.

Unless otherwise stated:

- Extensions may be granted only in exceptional cases and must be approved by the course staff.
- Requests for extensions should be made before the deadline whenever possible.

## Collaboration Policy

Students are encouraged to discuss course concepts, algorithms, and general problem-solving strategies with classmates.

However, each student must submit their own work.

Allowed collaboration may include:

- Discussing lecture material
- Explaining high-level ideas
- Debugging general programming issues
- Studying together

Not allowed:

- Copying another student’s code or written solution
- Sharing complete solutions
- Submitting work produced by another person
- Using unauthorized solution repositories
- Allowing another student to submit your work

---

## Academic Integrity

Academic integrity is required in all parts of the course.

Violations may include, but are not limited to:

- Plagiarism
- Copying code or written answers
- Unauthorized collaboration
- Fabricating results
- Submitting someone else’s work
- Using hidden or unauthorized resources during exams

Any suspected violation may be reviewed by the course staff and handled according to university regulations.

## AI Tools Policy

AI tools such as ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, Claude, Gemini, or similar systems may be used only in ways that support learning and understanding.

Allowed uses may include:

- Clarifying concepts
- Getting help with debugging
- Improving code readability
- Brainstorming approaches
- Learning how to use libraries or tools

Not allowed:

- Submitting AI-generated solutions without understanding them
- Using AI tools to directly solve assignments when prohibited
- Copying generated code or text without proper review
- Presenting AI-generated work as entirely your own
- Using AI tools during exams unless explicitly allowed

Students are responsible for everything they submit.  
The course staff may ask students to explain their solutions.

When required by the course staff, students must disclose the use of AI tools in their submissions.
